What is the MTG Land Calculator?

This is a utility that calculates odds for a problem in the game Magic the Gathering.

It answers this question:
With a particular mana-base, what are the odds that I will have drawn the right lands to cast a specific spell on each of the first ten turns?

How do I use it?

1. Pick a card to consider casting.
For this example, let's talk about the card Bribery . It costs three colorless and two blue mana.
If you'd like you can follow along by clicking on the calculator tab above, and flipping back and forth from About to the Calculator.

2. Enter the card's cost where it says "What does your spell cost?".
For Bribery, you want a 2 for the blue mana box, and a 3 for the grey box, which represents colorless mana.

3. Enter your mana base in the area prompting "What lands are in your deck?".
A typical limited deck mana base might have 8 islands, 9 swamps. For this you'd put an 8 by the blue box, and a 9 by the black one.
If you had a land that could produce both black and blue, like Underground Sea you could put a one by the hybrid blue and black box.

4. Fill out other information, including the number of total cards in your deck, and hit "Get Your Odds!".
For our hypothetical limited deck we'd put down 40 cards.

5. A graph will appear showing how likely it is that you'll have drawn enough lands to play a spell on each of the first ten turns.
The odds are shown in decimal form, so .59 on turn 5 in this example means that 59/100 times with 8 islands and 9 swamps in a 40 card deck on the play, you'll be able to cast your Bribery on the 5th turn.

What assumptions does it make?

1. As of now this tool does not look at the odds of being able to cast the spell. It looks at the odds of drawing enough lands. This means that it will report the chance of having drawn the right lands to cast Bribery by turn 2 as greater than 0, when only two lands could have been played by then. The odds are close for being able to cast, and having drawn lands to cast.

2. It does not take into account non-land factors like ramp spells and card selection spells.
It operates as if you play one land per turn and do not cast any cards like Divination, which draws two cards, or Rampant Growth which puts a land into play.

3. It assumes that your lands tap for one mana and come into play untapped.
Some dual lands like Azorius Chancery do neither of these. For the most part your odds are quite close.

Why are these assumptions made?

Each of the assumptions greatly reduces the hardware muscle needed to get you a fast answer. If there is enough demand, future versions of the utility may support assumption-free calculations.
